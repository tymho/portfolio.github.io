Impact I want to have

Kindness is threefold. 1) Environment towards our health. 2) People towards the environment. 3) People towards each other. Technology takes care of the first and the second, while morals take care of the last. I will address each of the three directions. 1) I want to make healthcare humane. A patient suffering from chronic physical and mental pain, often exacerbated by human-made inefficiencies, should not have to be. Likewise, society should also be more empathetic towards the humans that participate in them. Unhappiness that stems from monotonous, low-growth jobs still exists, while the mental health crisis continues to be an epidemic. I want to reduce pain through optimizing entrenched societal processes towards user-friendliness and implementing humane automation. 2) It is unacceptable that species are disappearing and that temperatures are rising. Combining chemistry, physics, and biology, humans finally found how to address each carbon-contributing source. I see myself contributing through engineering hardware and biology while advocating for the best solutions. The effort is collective, and I need to know that we have tried our best to remedy the problems that were created. 3) Selfishness is omnipresent. From political gridlocks to corporate ladders, the root of society’s problem may not be technological and instead be moral. While I don’t have answers, I understand certain fundamentals of truth. Kindness is good. Cruelty is bad. I believe I am not the only one who understands, but I don’t believe everyone understands. The impact I want to make here is simple: I want people to understand.
